Correlations Discovered Between Health Indicators and Income and Age

The data points for Median Household Income, Median Age, and the state's rate of poverty were compared with data points for the percent of the population considered obese, the percentage of the population that smokes, and the percentage of the population that lacks access to healthcare.

When the poverty rate is compared to all obesity, smoking, and lack of healthcare rates, there is a strong correlation. When switching from the poverty rate to median household income, there is a mirror image of results, since a high poverty rate could result in a lower median income, and vice versa. When median age is compared to obesity, smoking and lack of healthcare rates, there is no correlation.
